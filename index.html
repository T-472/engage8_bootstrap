So You Wanna Hit Hawaii...

...but you don't know where to start? The attractions and appeal of the state of Hawaii are iconic and bear a bombastic reputation to denizens the world over, but could just as easily be overwhelming for newcomers. Stay tuned for some scenic site suggestions recommended by yours truly.

O'ahu Island

The locations to be listed on this site pertain to one of the Hawaiian islands, O'ahu, which notably houses Honolulu. Key locations to hit are named in the context of the visitor conducting their stay at Waikiki Beach and strike a moderate balance of both simple sight-seeing and working those glutes (you did achieve peak beach bod before coming here...right?)

Byodo-In Temple

Located a little ways near the northern side of O'ahu Island, this is a gorgeous temple located within the Valley of the Temples. Due to its distance, a rented mode of transportation would be highly advised and doubles as a means to access the next locations.

Waimea Valley

A sacred historical site near the western edge that triples as a laid-back trekking trail and biodiversity powerhouse. Witness the native flora and fauna that ends with pleasant miniature waterfall that visitors can also take a dip in.

Diamond Head Crater

An incredibly short drive from the main city center (through traffic that nearly mirrors that of Los Angeles), Diamond Head is an immensely popular climb to the top of the crater and presents a picturesque landscape view of Waikiki Beach and further beyond. The hike is testing, but certainly not insurmountable, so newbies need not be intimidated. Entry requires advance booking, so plan ahead!

Fort DeRussy (U.S. Army Museum of Hawaii)

Being a huge WWII enjoyer (particularly of the naval aspects), I just had to check out one of the historical sites on O'ahu Island. While perhaps not as infamous as Pearl Harbor or bearing an equal amount of significance, it is within walking distance of Waikiki beach. The free admission and lack of crowds make this location a very relaxing means of immersing in history.
